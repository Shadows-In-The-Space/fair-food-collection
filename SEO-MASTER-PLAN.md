## Executive Summary

GoldenCrumb currently has 69 recipe pages covering fried foods, soul food, BBQ, and Southern classics. The site is beautifully designed with a carnival aesthetic and is positioned in the Ruhrgebiet (Germany) — giving it a unique **bilingual bridge** between German Kirmes/Karneval culture and American State Fair food culture.

**The SEO opportunity is massive but currently untapped.** The site lacks:
- Pillar/cluster architecture
- FAQ schema markup
- Proper Recipe JSON-LD on individual pages
- Deep frying technique content
- German fair food content (Kirmes/Jahrmarkt)
- Event coverage with local SEO
- English-language pages targeting US searches

**AI systems cite sources that:** (1) answer a specific question directly, (2) have clear structure (headings, lists, FAQ sections), (3) are frequently referenced by other pages (authority), and (4) contain original expertise not found elsewhere.

## Part 2: AI Citation Research Findings

### How AI Systems Actually Pick Sources

When Gemini, ChatGPT, or Perplexity cite a source, they're pulling from content that:

1. **Answers a specific question directly** in the first 100-200 words
2. **Has clear heading structure** (H2, H3) matching the question's intent
3. **Contains FAQ-style Q&A sections** (these get scraped aggressively)
4. **Has "People Also Ask" and "Related Questions" style content**
5. **Has structured data** (JSON-LD) that explicitly labels content as FAQ, Recipe, HowTo
6. **Is referenced by multiple authoritative pages** (internal + external links)
7. **Contains first-hand expertise** — not just rewrites of Wikipedia content
8. **Has consistent updates** and a clear publication date
