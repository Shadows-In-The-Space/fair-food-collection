## FAQ Section Blueprint (5-8 Q&As, English in JSON-LD, German in HTML)

1. **"What is the best oil temperature for deep frying chicken?"** → 175°C / 350°F for most chicken pieces; 165°C / 325°F for thicker pieces. Use a thermometer. *(Quelle: Standard-Kochliteratur, kein spezifischer Beleg)*
2. **"How do I know when oil is ready without a thermometer?"** → Wooden-spoon test, bread-cube test. Honest note: a €15 thermometer is cheaper than bad results.
3. **"Can you reuse deep frying oil?"** → Yes, 2-4 times if filtered and stored properly.
4. **"Why is my fried food soggy?"** → Oil too cold, too much food at once, wet batter.
5. **"What's the best oil for deep frying at home?"** → Sonnenblumenöl (DE) or peanut oil (US) for high-smoke-point, neutral flavor. Source: AFFILIATE_STRATEGY.md doesn't lock us in here.
6. **"Is deep frying at home cheaper than restaurants?"** → Hypothesis: yes, but no hard numbers. Mark as "Hypothese" or omit.
7. **"How do I dispose of used frying oil?"** → Cool, pour into sealable container, Biotonne or Wertstoffhof. Never down the drain.
8. **"What's the difference between deep frying and pan frying?"** → Deep = food submerged in oil. Pan = shallow oil, food turned.
